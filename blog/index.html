Music is uncountable

Our project aims at highlighting songs spreading pattern in the world. Using a dataset from the Spotify website, containing the weekly top 100 songs rankings in 53 countries, we are able to show popularity timelines of songs around the world.

  • Get Started

Focus on a song

Our first objective was to create a visualization focusing on a song, highlighting how its popularity spreads around the world and how it faded.

We imagined displaying a histogram of weekly streams for the song, showing how its popularity evolved through the year.

To highlight the popularity according to the region we had the idea to display a world map with circles on the countries, with area varying according to the number of weekly streams, and inside the circle the ranking of the song in the country's chart.

First version - Overview

The first thing we did was to upload a map of the world. Then we added a research bar to allow the user to look for a song and select it. When the music is selected, the total number of streamings in each country is computed and the information displays when hovering a country.

First version - Number of streamings

When the user hover a country, the total number of streamings for the chosen music is displayed.

First version - Zoom and information on country

The user could also click on a country in the map, which triggers a zoom and let a window appear, suggesting to click on a button to display two rankings (one displaying the best songs and the other displaying the best artists in the country selected).

On the way to version 2

2-pages design

When focusing on a song, we wanted also to provide insight about its popularity. We decided to sort the countries by weekly streams and display a ranking.

We designed a visualization in two pages with:

  • The worldmap on top
  • The countries ranking on the bottom
This design aimed at allowing the user to switch from one page ton another easily by scrolling or clicking on a button.

A menu on the right would remain on screen at all time allowing the user to change the song to display.

Focus on a country

Appart from focusing on song, we also wanted to offer the possibility to highlight a specific country's music tastes, varying with time.

The idea is to zoom on the user's selected country on the map, and display image or titles of the most popular songs, with area varying according to the songs popularity. A timeline is also used to display the ranking according to the time of the year.

On the rank page, we would sort according to the number of streams in the selected country and display them, with their informations.

A menu on the right would remains on screen at all time allowing the user to change the country to display, or swith to song mode and vice-versa.

Toplist, toplist everywhere!

As can be seen on almost every picture above, there is a toplist. That is the second main part of our visualization. Who would imagine presenting songs without a toplist?!

Our first idea was to create a static toplist, as it can be seen on Spotify and many other websites. But that was to simple.

We decided to bring some life to it and make it move everytime a new toplist was generated. Basically user can compare most popular songs from different regions just by looking at the toplist. All transitions are based on difference between next and previous toplist. First records, that are missing in next toplist vanish, then songs present in both rankings update thier place and, in the end, new ones come with yellow colour as an indicator of being new.

New colours

Next step consisted of merging the toplist with the rest of visualization and changing colours.

We decided to stick with nice grey palette that fits the map very well.

Version 2 - Overview

For the second version of our website, we decided to have a table on the right of the map that allows the user to first choose whether they want information on countries or songs. Once the tab is clicked, the user can either select a song by taping and selecting it in the search bar or select a country from the list of country to display information.

We decided to trigger the zoom on a country when the user clicks on the name in the list instead of directly on the map to make it easier to select small countries like Switzerland. In our final version, we will see that we then decided to keep both possibilities (click on the name in the list and directly on the map).

Version 2 - Song selection

When a song is selected, a timeline representing the number of streamings over time of this song in each continent is displayed on the top of the map, at the bottom of the webpage. The user can then click on the arrow to go down in the website, where a table shows further information on the song. (In the picture, the elements of the table were not linked to the dataset yet and some random data was displayed just for testing.)

On the way to final visualization

1-page design

We decided that it would give a better user experience if everything was displayed on the same page, so we resized and repositioned the different elements of our previous versions to make them fit in the webpage.For that, we got rid of the arrow, we put the below table on the right of the map and we hided the list of countries when the mouse was not hovering it.

Song or Country mode

To keep interactivity with the map, we decided to re implement the zoom on a country by clicking directly on it in the map. However, to avoid confusion for the user, we decided to allow them to click on the map only if the tab "countries" is opened (the user is in "country mode").

Design and colors

Relying on design principles, we decided to change the colours of countries and use colors with the same saturation and brightness for each continent to make the comparison between them easier.

Radar plots

Spotify API provides a lot of information abouts songs. One of them are audio features.

Audio features describe songs with a comparable metrics like:

  • acousticness
  • danceability
  • energy
  • instrumentalness
  • etc.

The best way to present those information is to use radar plot.

Final Visualization

In our final visualization, everything is on the same page.

The worldmap is always shown on the left of the page and a menu on the right appears when the user hover on the right part of the screen. The menu allows to chose to look for a specific song or a specific country to get information on.

A few trendy and popular songs are proposed to the user in the "song" menu. He can simply click on one of them to easily get the informations about it.

Radar plot and toplist integration

Having two working modules we had to make them work together. Each song as of now has an information icon that, if hovered over, presents audio features.

User can have a general overview by looking at the area of the plot, but he can also inspect all values by hovering over each point.

Final Visualization

A full list of the 53 countries with Spotify is displayed in the "country" menu. When the user selected the country menu, it is also possible to click on a country on the map instead of chosing from the list.

Song visualization

After selecting a song, the menu close automatically and a histogram of the song's streams around the world appear, as well as a ranking summing up the number of streams of the song in the countries for all 2017.

This gives already a clear idea of how much popular the songs has been throughout the year, and in which countries it has been listened the most.

Playing the song timelapse

The play button on the song histogram allows the user to launch the time vizualisation, displaying the countries ranking week by week, and showing on the map the weekly popularity of the song in each country using colour opacity.

Country visualization

After selecting a country, the menu close automatically and a histogram of the country streams/week appears, as well as a ranking summing up the number of streams per songs in the country for all 2017.

This gives already a clear idea of what were the most popular songs in the country for the year, and how much spotify is used in the country.

Playing the country timelapse

The play button on the song histogram allows the user to launch the time vizualisation, displaying the countries ranking week by week, and showing on the map the weekly popularity of the song in each country using colour opacity.
